--- BRIEF SUMMARY ---
This Phase III, double-blind, randomized, placebo-controlled trial evaluates the efficacy and safety of semaglutide 2.4 mg once weekly compared to placebo for weight management in adults with overweight or obesity. The trial lasts 68 weeks, involving a 16-week dose-escalation phase and a 52-week maintenance phase.

--- DETAILED DESCRIPTION ---
Participants are randomized 2:1 to receive semaglutide (2.4 mg) or placebo subcutaneous injections once weekly. Both groups receive a lifestyle intervention (500 kcal/day deficit diet and 150 min/week physical activity). The simulation must account for the gradual titration from 0.25mg to 2.4mg to minimize GI adverse events.

--- PRIMARY ENDPOINTS ---
1. Percentage change in body weight from baseline to week 68.
2. Percentage of participants achieving body-weight reduction of at least 5% by week 68.

--- ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA ---
Inclusion Criteria:
* Age >= 18 years.
* BMI >= 30.0 kg/m² OR >= 27.0 kg/m² with >= 1 weight-related comorbidity (hypertension, dyslipidemia, obstructive sleep apnea, or cardiovascular disease).
* History of failed self-reported dietary weight loss efforts.

Exclusion Criteria:
* Type 1 or Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (HbA1c >= 6.5%).
* Change in body weight > 5 kg within 90 days before screening.
* History of pancreatitis.
* History of major depressive disorder or suicidal ideation.

--- WITHDRAWAL RULES ---
1. Pregnancy or intention to become pregnant.
2. Occurrence of Acute Pancreatitis.
3. Investigator discretion regarding safety (e.g., severe GI distress leading to malnutrition).
4. Participant withdrawal of consent.
5. Initiation of other weight-loss medications or bariatric surgery.
